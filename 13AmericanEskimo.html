American Eskimo

The American Eskimo Dog is a small to medium-sized companion and working dog breed that originated in the United States. Despite its name, it is not from Eskimo heritage but is descended from European Spitz-type dogs. This breed was popular in circuses and farms because of its intelligence, agility, and friendly nature.

American Eskimo Dogs have a thick, fluffy double coat that is usually pure white or white with biscuit cream markings. Their fox-like face, erect ears, and curled tail give them a beautiful and elegant appearance. They come in three sizes: Toy, Miniature, and Standard, but all share the same lively personality.

In terms of personality, the American Eskimo Dog is friendly, affectionate, and very loyal to its family. These dogs love being around people and enjoy playing with children. They are alert and make excellent watchdogs because they tend to bark when they notice something unusual.

This breed is highly intelligent and eager to please, which makes it easy to train. American Eskimo Dogs excel in obedience, agility, tricks, and dog sports. They enjoy learning new commands and performing tasks, especially when rewarded with praise and treats.

American Eskimo Dogs are active and need regular exercise such as daily walks, playtime, and mental stimulation. Their thick coat requires regular brushing to prevent matting and reduce shedding. Overall, the American Eskimo Dog is a cheerful, intelligent, and loving companion that thrives in active and caring homes.
